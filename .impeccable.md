# Florin — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
A single technical European household head (the owner is also the operator). They self-host Florin on their own hardware, value privacy, and want to see their financial reality at a glance — net worth, burn rate, runway, where the money went this month — without any of the upsell, gamification, or "scoring" baggage of consumer fintech apps. They open the dashboard quickly, often on mobile, and need numbers they trust. Locale defaults to French (`fr-FR`, `€`).

### Brand Personality
**Calm. Precise. Honest.**

The interface should feel like a well-built instrument panel: every number is exact, nothing dances for attention, nothing tries to make the user feel a particular way about their finances. Confidence comes from legibility and consistency, not from celebratory animations or color-coded mood-lifting. The tone is closer to Linear or a Bloomberg terminal than to a neobank app.

Emotional goals: **trust**, **clarity**, **quiet competence**. Never anxious, never "fun," never patronizing.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Reference feel:** Linear (density, restraint, monospace numerics, careful typography), Copilot Money (data-forward finance UI), Things (whitespace discipline), Notion (calm content surfaces). Fintech reference, not bank reference.
- **Anti-references:** Generic shadcn-default look, gamified neobank dashboards (Revolut-style confetti, "your spending is up 🔥"), neon crypto, stock-photo bank corporate, dense Excel grids without hierarchy, Material Design heavy elevation.
- **Density:** Linear-tight. Small but readable type, compact KPI cards, useful info above the fold. Whitespace is structural, not decorative.
- **Theme:** Both light and dark already wired via `next-themes`; **dark mode is the primary surface to optimize** (operator likely runs it 90% of the time), with light kept fully usable.
- **Color:** Indigo primary (`oklch(0.55 0.18 260)`), neutral base, restrained accent use. The five-color chart palette (blue / emerald / amber / rose / purple) is the *only* place colors should multiply — everywhere else, color carries meaning (positive=emerald, negative=destructive-rose, neutral=muted) or it stays out of the way.
- **Numerics:** Money values are tabular/monospace, right-aligned where they line up in columns, with `fr-FR` thousand separators and the `€` suffix. Negative amounts use a real minus sign and the destructive color, never parentheses.
- **Motion:** Minimal, purposeful. Fade and translate, never bounce. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Never animate a chart re-render — stale data flicker is worse than instant swap. (Recharts `isAnimationActive={false}` is already the convention here — keep it.)

### Design Principles
1. **The number is the product.** Typography hierarchy and tabular alignment matter more than decoration. If a card has a big number, that number wins the visual weight contest — labels, deltas, and chrome shrink to support it.
2. **Color is meaning, not decoration.** Reserve hue for status (positive / negative / warning) and chart series. Surfaces stay neutral. If you can't justify a color in one sentence, remove it.
3. **Density with breathing room.** Pack information, but never crowd. Use a consistent spacing scale (4 / 8 / 12 / 16 / 24 / 32) and let related items cluster tighter than unrelated ones. Section gaps > card gaps > content gaps.
4. **Quiet by default, loud only on demand.** No animation on idle, no hover effects that compete with reading, no badges that don't communicate state. Interactions reveal themselves only when the cursor or focus arrives.
5. **Consistency beats cleverness.** One way to render a money value. One way to render a date. One filter pill style. One empty state pattern. The dashboard should feel like one designer made every screen on the same Tuesday.
